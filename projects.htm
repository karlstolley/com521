Projects

  1. Ongoing Projects

    • Professional Discussion: Choosing either a listserv (e.g., ATTW-L, CPTSC) to follow or following academics in technical communication and related fields on Twitter; posting and participation is encouraged, but not required.
    • Classroom Discussion: Actively engaging in weekly discussion, responding to peer work and presentations (30pts)

  2. Minor Projects

    • Background Article: Choosing one key article cited by an assigned reading in Central Works in Technical Communication and preparing a digital handout for the class that includes the citation, the connection to Central Works, plus an outline, key quote, and summary of the article and delivering a max. 10-minute presentation on it to the class. See Stolleyâ€™s example. (Note: Your background article must not itself appear in Central Works.) (10pts)
    • Conference Proposal: An individual paper proposal (or panel proposal, if multiple students wish to collaborate), following the specific call for papers (CFP) of an actual technical communication or related conference: ATTW, IEEE/IPCC, CPTSC, etc. Alternatively, students may write an article proposal in response to a CFP for a special issue of a journal or an edited book collection. (draft due 9/12, final due 9/19; 10pts)

  3. Major Projects

    • Annotated Bibliography: Covers the past 5-7 years of a specific topic in technical communication or related fields, one that should be leading towards a possible dissertation topic or MS project; 20 key sources (articles, chapters, and books) with paragraph-long annotations. (draft due 10/3, final due 10/24; 25pts)
    • Article-Length Literature Review/Bibliographic Essay: Approaching publishable length & quality and based on the Annotated Bibliography; drafted with a specific technical communication journal in mind and according to that journal's submission guidelines and citation/formatting style. Should frame a particular argument for placing and understanding the last 5-7 years of research, and likely suggests or forecasts future research directions in the topic area. (draft due 11/21, final due 12/5; 25pts)
